# 4.1 Your Agentic PMM Starter Kit

The AI-First PMM vision is exciting. It's also a little overwhelming when you're trying to ship a launch, keep Sales happy, and figure out why the last campaign underperformed.

Here's the thing: you don't need to redesign your entire function to start. You just need to actually ... start.

Five things you can do this week, with what you already have, to begin the journey. No budget approval required. No team restructure.

## 1. Write Your Context Files

This is the foundation of everything. AI is only as good as the context you give it. And right now, most of that context exists in your head, in scattered slide decks, and in email threads nobody can find.

Start by writing your MSD/MPF in Markdown. Plain text, structured JSON that clearly states your ICP, your personas, your "why now" narrative, your proof points.

Once this exists as a file your AI can ingest, everything changes. Every draft it generates, every persona it interviews, every stress-test it runs, it's working from *your* truth, not a generic approximation of it.

This is the single highest-leverage thing you can do. Do it first. Do it now.

## 2. Replace One Onerous Manual Task

Don't try to automate everything at once. Pick one thing you do manually and repetitively, maybe competitive monitoring, synthesising win/loss notes, drafting persona summaries, and build one simple AI workflow around it.

The goal isn't perfection. The goal is proof. Prove to yourself that the machine can handle the 80% on this one task, and that you can do the 20% review faster than doing the whole thing manually. Once you have that proof, the next workflow becomes much easier.

## 3. Establish Zero-Trust as a Habit

Say it with me: AI is a vibe engine, not a database.

Nothing AI-generated goes anywhere near a customer without manual verification. Every technical claim. Every stat. Every proof point. This is not a lack of trust in the tools, it's the professional standard. The PMM is the source of truth. Always.

Build this habit before something slips through. Your credibility is not worth the time saved by skipping the check.

## 4. Map Your 20%

Spend deliberate time on what AI *can't* do for you.

Your read on internal politics. Your instinct about why a message lands differently with enterprise buyers versus SMB. The real-world context that lives in a conversation, a hallway, a candid moment after a customer call.

Write these down. Not because AI will take them, but because knowing your 20% helps you protect it. It's where your value lives. Don't accidentally automate it away by defaulting to the machine on every decision.

## 5. Stress-Test Your Messaging Before Your Buyers Do

Take your current messaging and give an AI a simple instruction: *"You are a skeptical enterprise buyer evaluating this product against three alternatives. Interview this messaging framework and tell me where it falls apart."*

You won't enjoy every answer. That's the point. Better to find the gaps in a chat window than in a customer conversation.

This is the Humans + AI model at its most practical. AI finding the holes, you deciding which ones matter and how to fix them.
